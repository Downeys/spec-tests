## Core thesis

> Most AI knowledge tools spend compute and tokens to **rederive**. Karpathy's wiki **compiles**. That's a fundamentally different relationship between AI and your information — different strengths and weaknesses than other memory paradigms (including OpenBrain).

## Two systems compared

### Karpathy wiki (write-time synthesis)

- AI is primarily a **writer** at ingest.
- Reads source → extracts what matters → writes/updates wiki pages → links related ideas → flags contradictions.
- Heavy work at input; cheap retrieval afterward (you just read the synthesis).
- **Wins when:** deep research mode, evolving understanding over weeks/months, value lives in connections between sources, solo practitioner.
- **Breaks at:** scale (~100–10,000 documents), multi-agent simultaneous writes (markdown merge conflicts), high-velocity operational data (Slack/tickets), structured queries ("show me every meeting where pricing was discussed").
- **Failure mode:** stale wiki = active misinformation that *reads with confidence*. Old syntheses become wrong but the page sounds authoritative.

### OpenBrain (query-time synthesis)

- AI is primarily a **reader** at query.
- Ingest = cheap: tag a row, store a row, done.
- Heavy work at query: read structured rows, synthesize fresh.
- **Wins when:** precise structured operations ("pull every Q1 meeting tagged pricing"), multi-agent access (multiple AIs writing/reading concurrently), volume (thousands+), faithful provenance ("this is the raw material I got, here's the source").
- **Breaks at:** deep synthesis quality (15-fact synthesis is unpredictable without pre-built map), browsability (headless by default), contradiction surfacing (DB is not contradiction-aware unless you specifically query for it).
- **Failure mode:** staleness looks like ignorance — gaps are visible because the answer says "I don't have that," not a confident-but-wrong page.

## The fundamental fork

> Every knowledge system with an AI at its core has to answer one question: when does the AI do the hard thinking? At write-time (when info comes in) or at query-time (when you ask)? You have to pick.

## Strategic implications

- **Wiki staleness vs. DB staleness** matter very differently. DB staleness = visible gaps. Wiki staleness = confident-sounding misinformation.
- **Contradictions** are sometimes the most valuable signal in a knowledge base. A wiki may smooth them away ("eng says 12 weeks, sales promised 8 → wiki says 10 weeks") and you lose the strategic signal of misalignment. A structured DB preserves both views unresolved.
- **Provenance:** with OpenBrain, you can trace any claim to its source row. With wiki, you're trusting the AI's editorial choice. *"The instructions you give the AI that tells it how to organize your wiki becomes the highest leverage document in the whole system."*
- **AI as writer vs. reader:** wiki = AI is a writer (editorial judgement). OpenBrain = AI is a reader (analytical fresh synthesis from structured facts).

## Common principles both share

- You own the artifact, not the tool (file-over-app, no SaaS middleman).
- Human's job is curation + questioning; AI does the bookkeeping/synthesis.
- Memory compounds through *intentional structure*, not random accumulation.
- The primary user of the knowledge base is increasingly the AI agent, not the human reader. Human readability is a bonus; agent accessibility is the requirement.

## The hybrid (what Nate is building / what user wants)

This is the architecture the user has chosen for this project.

> "Keep OpenBrain as your permanent store. Don't change that. Every meeting note, every article clip, every research finding, every task, every contact, it's all tagged, searchable, queryable. That is your durable memory layer."
>
> "A wiki layer can act as a compiled view on demand. A compilation agent runs on a schedule — daily, weekly, on demand. The agent reads from OpenBrain structured data (effectively becomes an OpenBrain graph), synthesizes across entries, produces wiki pages on demand, produces topic summaries — all driven by the idea that if you form a graph of your knowledge base, you can get the advantages of the wiki approach with the solidity and factuality that comes from an OpenBrain SQL database."

### Layered architecture (hybrid)

| Layer | Role | Write pattern | Read pattern |
|-------|------|---------------|--------------|
| **OneBrain (SQL/Postgres)** | Source of truth — every fact, source, finding, transaction, decision | Cheap, multi-agent safe, tagged + categorized + queryable rows | Precise structured queries, filter by date/category/confidence, exclude outdated items |
| **Compilation agent** | Read OneBrain → synthesize → produce wiki pages | Scheduled (daily/weekly) and/or on-demand | Drives the wiki refresh |
| **Karpathy wiki (Obsidian markdown)** | Compiled, navigable, narrative artifact with cross-references and contradictions surfaced | Generated artifact (not hand-edited; not directly written by chat) | Browse in Obsidian; surfaced as markdown chunks in chat UI |

### Why hybrid wins for this project

- Wiki narrative + cross-reference + contradiction-flagging strengths.
- OpenBrain's scale, multi-agent safety, faithful provenance, structured query.
- Wiki pages are generated artifacts — *the database stays the single source of truth*. New information always goes into the database first.
- Synthesis can filter by date/category/confidence/freshness *before* compiling — so the wiki is richer than a wiki built from raw files alone.

## Key design implications for our build

1. **Write path:** new research → OneBrain (structured, tagged) **first**, never directly into the wiki.
2. **Compilation agent:** distinct agent (or scheduled job) that reads OneBrain and emits/updates Obsidian markdown. Can run on schedule + on demand.
3. **Provenance / evidence linking:** every wiki claim links back to specific OneBrain rows (via stable IDs). This is the "evidence link back to OneBrain" requirement from PROJECT.md.
4. **Confidence weighting:** wiki synthesis can weight or filter by confidence stored in OneBrain rows.
5. **Contradiction surfacing:** explicitly preserved — compilation agent flags contradictions rather than smoothing them away (that was the explicit failure mode Nate called out).
6. **Multi-agent safe:** sub-agents (research, financial analysis, etc.) can all write to OneBrain concurrently without merge conflicts. Wiki regeneration is single-writer.
7. **Critical/hypothesis behavior:** every OneBrain row carries a confidence + status (hypothesis | tested | validated | refuted). Compilation agent reflects that in wiki narrative.
