We surveyed 412 independent restaurant operators (under $1M annual revenue) about their scheduling and operations workflows. The results paint a picture of an underserved segment that's still running on spreadsheets, group texts, and gut feel — even as enterprise restaurants move toward AI-driven workforce platforms.

## Key findings

1. **62% of independent restaurants under $1M revenue manage scheduling manually** — typically a printed weekly schedule plus a group SMS thread for shift swaps. Only 14% use a dedicated scheduling tool.

2. **SMS is the dominant channel for ops alerts.** When asked how they prefer to receive shift-change notifications, 71% chose SMS, 18% phone call, 9% email, 2% other. Email was rated "actively unhelpful" by 34% of respondents — operators report not checking email during service hours.

3. **Owner-operators average 11 hours/week on scheduling and shift-swap coordination.** This is roughly equivalent to one part-time labor cost they aren't tracking. The 11-hour figure is consistent across geographies and cuisines.

4. **Most SMB owners use a single POS** — 78% reported one primary POS; 17% reported two; 5% reported three or more. This contradicts the prevailing industry narrative that SMB restaurants have fragmented payment infrastructure. (Note: respondents skewed toward dine-in/quick-service; ghost-kitchen operators were under-represented.)

5. **Adoption blockers, in order:** price (61%), perceived complexity (54%), lack of integration with their POS (48%), training time for staff (39%), trust in the vendor (22%).

## Pricing context

Of operators using a paid scheduling tool, the median monthly spend was $58 per location. The 25th percentile was $24/mo (typically a freemium tier). The 75th percentile was $129/mo (mid-market tools with integrations).

## Methodology note

Sample frame: independent restaurants under $1M annual revenue, US-based, English-speaking. Recruited via industry newsletter list and POS partner referrals. Response rate 23%. Margin of error ±4.8% at 95% confidence. Full instrument and dataset available on request.
